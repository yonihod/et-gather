# Design Context

## Target Audience
~70-80 Israeli gamers aged 30-45 who played RTCW: Enemy Territory competitively ~20 years ago. They're nostalgic, community-driven, and reconnecting after decades. Mix of casual and competitive players. Hebrew-first, tech-savvy.

## Use Cases
- Organize and join 6v6 gather matches
- Track attendance and see who's most active
- Find community links (Discord, Telegram, server IP)
- Download game configs and setup instructions

## Brand Personality/Tone
**Military-nostalgic meets modern competitive gaming.** Not corporate, not childish. Think: a veteran clan's war room — serious about the game, but warm and welcoming. The community logo features crossed rifles, Israeli flags, and a helmeted mascot — gritty, patriotic, proud.

## Aesthetic Direction
**Retro-military meets modern esports.** Warm dark tones (not cold/techy dark), olive/khaki accents alongside the green primary. Typography should feel strong and purposeful. The vibe is a WWII briefing room modernized — think aged paper textures conceptually, but executed with clean modern UI. Left-aligned, asymmetric where possible. No generic SaaS look.
